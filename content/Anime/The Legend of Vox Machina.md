# Season 01
I just finished Season 1. Initially, I didn't like the show's "unserious" tone—especially after watching _The Mighty Nein_first—but I eventually grew to enjoy it. The two shows are actually quite similar. I loved that the writers didn't sideline anyone in the main cast; during the final showdown, every character had a specific purpose and a moment to shine.

**Rating: 5/4.5**
- **4.5:** Perfectly captures the characters and delivers addictive hooks at the end of every episode.
- **0.5:** Occasionally uses forced humor during moments that should have stayed serious.
 
# Season 02
First off, when the dragons appeared, my first thought was: "Didn’t they defeat a dragon already?" Since it was hinted that blue dragons are more powerful than red ones, I was surprised. However, the show immediately raised the stakes by having the Chroma Conclave destroy the capital of Tal'Dorei while our heroes could do nothing but flee.

I loved that the story returned to Whitestone instead of sidelining it. The "power-ups" and character development across these two seasons were fantastic, and I appreciated how most of the characters' backstories were explored. While I hoped they would kill all four dragons this season, I’m glad the show finally delivered the seriousness I was craving.

On a side note: OMG, Scanlan trying to flirt with his own daughter... 😭💔 That song, "When the Bald Man Cries," really hit hard. 🎸

**Rating: 5/4.6**
- **4.6:** for the compelling backstories and for not undermining serious moments with poorly timed jokes.
- **0.4:** deducted because the final battle lacked impact. When the Briarwoods died, the people of Whitestone were finally free; however, Umbrasil’s death didn't feel like it changed the world in a meaningful way yet.

# Season 03
The season is off to a great start, and the humor is landing perfectly. I loved how in episode one, first thing we saw was Vax’s plan failing and everyone go on to predict how Vax would fail, and then we watched it happen exactly as they said—it was hilarious. I’m also impressed with Scanlan this season; he finally feels like a genuine character with a real arc instead of just being the comic relief. It’s a relief to see that they aren't rushing into the fight with Thordak without proper power-ups and Vestiges. Regarding Percy, I knew he was likely to return, but I hoped he stayed dead long enough for it to feel impactful. They rushed his revival to be honest.

**Rating: 5/4.8**
 - **4.8:** is for Scanlan’s development and the team's progression.  
 - **0.2:** is because I feel Percy’s 'death' needs more weight to feel meaningful."
