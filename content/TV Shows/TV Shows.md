---
title: TV Shows
---
I've always loved how TV shows make character arcs so visible over multiple seasons. **Loki’s** journey in the MCU is the perfect example; he started as a jealous prince and a literal 'villain' who tried to conquer Earth, only to end his story as a protector who sacrificed everything to hold the multiverse together.

Seeing villains change is my favorite part of storytelling. TV is the best medium for exploring complex redemption arcs—especially when an antagonist is allowed to grow and do good without being immediately killed off for 'shock value.' It gives the character time to breathe, fail, and eventually find a new purpose.
